## Abstract

This document defines the data residency and compliance policy for the ANIF autonomous networking pipeline. It establishes data residency as a first-class constraint (P-11), specifies the `region` constraint in the intent schema, defines approved regions per service type, details cross-region operation restrictions, specifies PCI-DSS and GDPR compliance requirements, mandates encryption requirements for sensitive contexts, defines audit data retention periods, and describes where data residency checks sit within the ANIF decision pipeline.

---

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

Autonomous network operations generate, process, and act upon data that may be subject to jurisdictional data protection requirements. An autonomous system that reroutes traffic, isolates segments, or scales bandwidth can inadvertently move sensitive data across regulatory boundaries if data residency is not treated as a first-class operational constraint. This policy ensures that ANIF:

- Evaluates data residency requirements before executing any action.
- Enforces region constraints declared in intent submissions.
- Satisfies PCI-DSS, GDPR, and other applicable compliance regimes.
- Retains audit records for the periods required by each applicable regime.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:

- All intents submitted to ANIF that include a `region` field.
- All actions executed by ANIF that may affect data routing or storage.
- All ANIF pipeline components that process, store, or transmit data.
- All audit records generated by the ANIF pipeline.

### 1.3 Out of Scope

- Data residency requirements for ANIF component infrastructure (governed by cloud/hosting policies).
- Cross-border data transfer agreements not related to network automation (legal department responsibility).
- Data classification schemes (assumed to be provided by the organisation).
- Personal data processing beyond what is captured in audit records.

### 1.4 Intended Audience

| Audience                | Purpose                                                                   |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Compliance Officer      | Primary owner; verify policy enforcement; produce compliance evidence     |
| Policy Administrator    | Configure region-specific policies; maintain approved region list          |
| Network Engineer        | Understand region constraints when submitting intents                     |
| Senior Engineer         | Approve cross-region exceptions; understand encryption requirements        |

## 4. Data Residency and Compliance Policy

### 4.1 Data Residency as a First-Class Constraint (P-11)

4.1.1 ANIF treats data residency as a first-class operational constraint pursuant to P-11. No action MUST be executed that would violate the data residency constraints declared in the intent.

4.1.2 The `region` field in the ANIF intent schema is a binding constraint. The pipeline MUST evaluate data residency during the policy evaluation stage, before risk scoring. A data residency violation MUST result in immediate rejection of the intent with `outcome: blocked` and `reason: data_residency_violation`.

4.1.3 The pipeline MUST NOT proceed past the policy evaluation stage if the target region is not in the approved list for the service type. This check is non-negotiable and MUST NOT be bypassed by any governance mode.

4.1.4 Intents that do not include a `region` field MUST be treated as if they specified the most restrictive applicable region. If no default region can be determined from context, the intent MUST be rejected with `reason: region_not_specified`.

### 4.2 Approved Regions

4.2.1 Approved regions are defined per service type in the policy store. The policy_administrator is responsible for maintaining the approved region list.

4.2.2 Default approved region matrix:

| Service Type        | EU   | US   | APAC | Cross-Region  |
|---------------------|------|------|------|---------------|
| General network     | Yes  | Yes  | Yes  | With approval |
| pci_compliant       | Yes  | Yes  | No   | Prohibited    |
| GDPR-sensitive      | Yes  | No   | No   | Prohibited    |
| Internal management | Yes  | Yes  | Yes  | With approval |

4.2.3 The policy_administrator MAY add or remove regions from the approved list for a service type. Any change to the approved region list MUST be logged in the audit trail.

4.2.4 Additions to the approved region list for `pci_compliant` or `GDPR-sensitive` service types MUST be approved by the compliance_officer before taking effect.

### 4.3 Data Residency Policy Evaluation

4.3.1 The data residency check MUST occur during the policy evaluation stage of the pipeline. It MUST be evaluated before risk scoring to ensure that residency violations are caught early and do not consume unnecessary compute.

4.3.2 The `data_residency` policy evaluation procedure:

```
1. Extract `region` from intent schema
2. Determine service_type from intent context
3. Look up approved_regions for service_type in policy store
4. IF region NOT IN approved_regions:
   → outcome: blocked
   → reason: data_residency_violation
   → audit record written
   → pipeline halts
5. ELSE:
   → proceed to next policy check
```

4.3.3 Cross-region operations require explicit approval. An intent that would result in traffic or data traversing regions MUST either:
- Be rejected if cross-region is prohibited for the service type, OR
- Route to `manual_review` with the compliance_officer designated as required approver.

4.3.4 The pipeline MUST NOT execute any action that would route pci_compliant or GDPR-sensitive traffic outside its declared region, even if approved by a senior_engineer. Such exceptions REQUIRE compliance_officer approval and MUST be logged.

### 4.4 PCI-DSS Compliance Requirements

4.4.1 Intents and actions involving segments, services, or flows tagged `pci_compliant` MUST satisfy all applicable PCI-DSS v4.0 requirements. At minimum:

4.4.2 Encryption requirement: all pci_compliant traffic MUST have `encryption: true` set. The pipeline MUST reject any intent involving a pci_compliant service where `encryption` is not explicitly set to `true`. Rejection reason: `pci_encryption_required`.

4.4.3 Data residency: pci_compliant data MUST NOT leave the declared region. Cross-region operations for pci_compliant services are PROHIBITED (see Section 4.2.2).

4.4.4 Access control: actions affecting pci_compliant segments MUST be authorised by network_engineer or higher roles. automation_agent roles MUST NOT independently execute actions on pci_compliant segments.

4.4.5 Audit retention: audit records for pci_compliant operations MUST be retained for a minimum of 12 months readily accessible and 3 years in total. (See Section 4.7 for full retention schedule.)

4.4.6 Change management: all changes to pci_compliant segments MUST be classified as normal changes (never standard/pre-authorised), requiring risk assessment and governance gate evaluation.

4.4.7 PCI compliance policy summary:

| PCI-DSS Requirement        | ANIF Control                                                      |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Req 1.2: Network controls  | Bounded action set; `isolate_segment` requires manual_review      |
| Req 2.2: System hardening  | ANIF-404 operational security controls                            |
| Req 3.3: Protect stored data | Encryption: true enforced for pci_compliant intents             |
| Req 4.2: Encrypt in transit | Encryption: true enforced; cross-region prohibited               |
| Req 7: Access restriction  | Role-based authorisation (ANIF-103, ANIF-205)                    |
| Req 10: Log and monitor    | ANIF-107 mandatory audit trail at every stage                    |
| Req 10.5: Audit log retention | 12 months accessible; 3 years archive (Section 4.7)            |
| Req 12.3: Risk assessment  | ANIF-307 risk scoring integrated into pipeline                    |

### 4.5 GDPR and Privacy Requirements

4.5.1 Intents that involve network segments or flows processing EU personal data MUST comply with GDPR requirements. The `region: EU` designation in the intent schema activates GDPR-specific policy checks.

4.5.2 GDPR data handling requirements enforced by ANIF:

- EU-region data MUST NOT be routed to non-EU regions by any autonomous action.
- Actions that involve `reroute_traffic` for GDPR-sensitive flows MUST verify that the target path remains within the EU region.
- `isolate_segment` for EU-region segments containing personal data MUST include a data impact note in the intent (field: `data_impact_assessment`).

4.5.3 Personal data in audit records: ANIF audit records MAY contain operator identities (`operator_id`) which constitute personal data under GDPR. Audit record data MUST be stored within the EU region for EU-region deployments and MUST NOT be replicated to non-EU regions without explicit data processing agreement.

4.5.4 Data minimisation: audit records MUST NOT contain raw network payload data. Only metadata (flow identifiers, segment IDs, action parameters) MUST be recorded. Actual packet or payload content MUST NOT appear in any ANIF record.

4.5.5 Right of erasure: ANIF audit records are subject to retention requirements (Section 4.7) that supersede erasure requests during the mandatory retention period. After the retention period, records MAY be purged. The compliance_officer is responsible for managing erasure requests that fall outside retention windows.

### 4.6 Encryption Requirements

Encryption MUST be enforced in the following circumstances:

| Context                                          | Encryption Requirement               | Enforced By                   |
|--------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| pci_compliant service (any region)               | MUST: `encryption: true` in intent   | Policy evaluation stage       |
| prod environment + EU region (any service)       | MUST: `encryption: true` in intent   | Policy evaluation stage       |
| GDPR-sensitive flows                             | MUST: `encryption: true` in intent   | Policy evaluation stage       |
| Audit records in transit                         | MUST: TLS 1.2 or higher              | ANIF-107, ANIF-404            |
| Audit records at rest (prod)                     | MUST: AES-256 or equivalent          | ANIF-107, ANIF-404            |
| Approval tickets in transit                      | MUST: TLS 1.2 or higher              | ANIF-104, ANIF-404            |
| Cross-region data transfer (when approved)       | MUST: end-to-end encryption          | ANIF-303                      |

If an intent violates an encryption requirement, the pipeline MUST reject the intent at the policy evaluation stage with `reason: encryption_required` and the specific context that triggered the requirement.

### 4.7 Audit Data Retention

Audit records generated by the ANIF pipeline are subject to the following minimum retention periods, determined by the compliance regime applicable to the operation:

| Compliance Regime     | Applicable Operations                    | Minimum Retention Period   | Format Requirement          |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| General operations    | All non-regulated operations             | 1 year readily accessible  | ANIF-107 schema              |
| GDPR                  | EU-region operations                     | 3 years                    | ANIF-107 schema; pseudonymised operator IDs recommended |
| PCI-DSS               | pci_compliant operations                 | 12 months accessible + 3 years archive | ANIF-107 schema; immutable |
| Financial services    | Operations on financial network segments | 7 years                    | ANIF-107 schema; immutable; hash-chained |
| SOX (if applicable)   | US-region financial operations           | 7 years                    | ANIF-107 schema; immutable  |
| Recommended minimum   | All operations (absent specific regime)  | 7 years                    | ANIF-107 schema              |

4.7.1 Where multiple compliance regimes apply to an operation, the LONGEST retention period MUST be used.

4.7.2 Audit records MUST NOT be deleted before their retention period expires. The pipeline MUST NOT expose a delete endpoint for audit records.

4.7.3 Archive tier records (beyond readily-accessible period) MUST remain recoverable within 24 hours.

4.7.4 At the end of the retention period, records MAY be purged. Purge operations MUST themselves be logged in a separate purge audit log retained for 3 years.

## 5. Conformance Requirements

5.1 Conformant implementations MUST evaluate the `region` constraint during the policy evaluation stage before risk scoring.

5.2 Conformant implementations MUST reject intents with no region specified where context cannot determine a default region.

5.3 Conformant implementations MUST enforce `encryption: true` for pci_compliant services and prod+EU combinations.

5.4 Conformant implementations MUST NOT expose delete endpoints for audit records.

5.5 Conformant implementations MUST apply the longest applicable retention period when multiple compliance regimes apply.

5.6 Cross-region operations for pci_compliant or GDPR-sensitive services MUST be prohibited unless compliance_officer approval is obtained and logged.

5.7 Implementations MUST NOT store raw packet or payload data in audit records.

---

## 6. Security Considerations

6.1 Data residency enforcement is a security control as well as a compliance control. Bypassing data residency checks represents both a regulatory violation and a potential data exfiltration vector.

6.2 Encryption requirements for pci_compliant services MUST be enforced in the pipeline and MUST NOT rely solely on operator compliance. The pipeline MUST validate that encryption is declared before execution.

6.3 Audit records themselves MUST be stored in compliance with data residency requirements. For EU-region deployments, the audit log store MUST be located in the EU region.

6.4 Access to audit records containing personal data (operator_id fields) MUST be restricted to compliance_officer and policy_administrator roles for the purposes of regulatory reporting. Network engineers MUST NOT have direct read access to raw audit records in production.

6.5 Cross-region operation approvals MUST be time-limited and logged. Standing approvals for cross-region operations MUST NOT be granted.

---

## 7. Operational Considerations

7.1 The compliance_officer SHOULD review the approved region list quarterly and adjust based on changes in the organisation's regulatory environment.

7.2 PCI-DSS compliance assessments (QSA audits) MUST include a review of ANIF audit records for pci_compliant segment operations. The compliance_officer MUST be able to produce a complete audit trail for any pci_compliant operation on demand.

7.3 Audit record retention infrastructure MUST be planned ahead of time. Operators SHOULD estimate audit volume based on intent submission rates and provision storage with sufficient capacity for the maximum retention period.

7.4 When adding a new region to the ANIF deployment, the compliance_officer MUST perform a regulatory assessment before the region is added to the approved region list for any regulated service type.

7.5 GDPR data subject access requests that touch ANIF audit records MUST be coordinated between the compliance_officer and the ANIF system administrator. Response time MUST comply with GDPR requirements (30 days, extendable to 90 days).

## Appendix A: Examples

### A.1 Data Residency Check — Intent Rejection

Intent: `reroute_traffic` for service tagged `GDPR-sensitive`; `region: US`.

1. Policy evaluation: `region: US` checked against approved regions for GDPR-sensitive service.
2. Approved regions for GDPR-sensitive: `[EU]`. US is not in the list.
3. Pipeline output: `outcome: blocked`, `reason: data_residency_violation`.
4. Audit record written at policy evaluation stage.
5. Requester notified; no further pipeline stages executed.

### A.2 PCI-DSS Encryption Enforcement

Intent: `apply_qos` for segment tagged `pci_compliant`; `encryption` field omitted.

1. Policy evaluation: service type = pci_compliant.
2. Encryption check: `encryption` field not present or not `true`.
3. Pipeline output: `outcome: blocked`, `reason: encryption_required`, `context: pci_compliant_service`.
4. Audit record written.
5. Requester notified to resubmit with `encryption: true`.

### A.3 Retention Period Application

Operation: `isolate_segment` on a segment that processes both EU personal data (GDPR) and payment card data (PCI-DSS).

1. GDPR retention requirement: 3 years.
2. PCI-DSS retention requirement: 3 years archive (12 months accessible).
3. Financial services regime (if applicable): 7 years.
4. Longest period applies: **7 years** if financial services regime applies; **3 years** otherwise.
5. Audit records retained and immutable for the applicable period.
